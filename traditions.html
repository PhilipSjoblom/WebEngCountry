Traditions

Día de los Muertos (Day of the Dead)

Mexico has a variety of eye-catching traditions filling the streets with colors like de, Día de los Muertos (Day of the Dead) where on the first and second of November they honor their loved ones and relatives. An important of (the Day of the Dead) is making alters and shrines to commemorate the dead with music, food, and skull-like face painting.

Las Posadas

Have you ever tried partying for 9 days straight? If you haven't but want to try, come down to Mexico where from 16-24 of December. You go from house to house smashing pinatas and eating different local delicacies, singing traditional songs etc.

Cinco de Mayo

Although not widely celebrated in Mexico as it is in the United States, Cinco de Mayo commemorates the Mexican victory over French forces at the Battle of Puebla on May 5, 1862. In Puebla, there are reenactments, parades, and festive activities.
